The 2D heat equation is a fundamental partial differential equation used to describe the diffusion of heat in space over time. It is given by:

\[
\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2}
\]

This equation states that the rate of change of the temperature \( u \) with respect to time is equal to the sum of the second derivatives of \( u \) with respect to space variables \( x \) and \( y \). The term on the right-hand side represents the Laplacian of \( u \), which measures the tendency of heat to diffuse from higher-temperature regions to lower-temperature regions.
